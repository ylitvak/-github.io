Visuospatial reasoning is reasoning with visuospatial knowledge. This
has two parts to it, visual and spatial. Visual deals with the what
part. Spatial deals with the where part. So imagine a picture in which
there is a sun on the top right of the picture. There are two parts to
it. Sun, the what, the object. And where, the top right of the picture.
We have come across visuospatial reasoning a little bit when we use
constraint propagation to do line labeling and 2D images. One way of
defining visuospatial knowledge is to say that in visuospatial knowledge
causality is, at most, implicit. Imagine a picture in which there is a
cup with a pool of water around it. You donâ€™t know where the pool of
water came from. But you and I can quickly infer that the cup must have
contained the water, and the water must have spilled out as the cup
fell. So visuospatial knowledge, causality is implicit when it enables
inferences about causality.
