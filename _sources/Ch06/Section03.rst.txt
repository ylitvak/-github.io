|Exercise A Pitcher 2| 
David, it’s clear that you know more about baseball than I do. So I
assume that your answer is the right one. But notice what is happening
here. David has a lot of knowledge about baseball, and he’s using that
knowledge to make a decision. How is he using his knowledge to make a
decision? What is the architecture? What is the reasoning that leads him
to make that specific decision? This is one of the things we’ll learn in
this lesson. How might intelligent agents make complex decisions about
the world?
